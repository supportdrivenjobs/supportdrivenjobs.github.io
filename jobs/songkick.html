Songkick Customer Support Manager

Songkick is the world's largest artist-ticketing platform and leading concert discovery service. We help artists sell more tickets on their own site, on Songkick and across a wide range of partners, including Spotify, YouTube and BBC Music. More than 10 million fans use us each month across web and mobile. We are the biggest independent concert site in the world. We have sold millions of tickets direct to fans and sell more than 10,000 events each year. Artists who use our ticketing service include Adele, Kenny Chesney, Ellie Goulding, John Legend, Paul McCartney, Metallica, Mumford & Sons, and The xx.

Our team combines deep consumer product and technical expertise with a complete view of what matters to modern artists. We are veterans of Apple, Skype, Google, and Amazon working alongside a former member of Beyonce’s management team, a producer for the Wu-Tang Clan, various working musicians, and the person who literally wrote the book on the ticketing industry.

Our global team is spread across offices strategically located in London, New York, Los Angeles and Nashville.

What we’re looking for

We're after someone who is driven by helping people, making them happy and most importantly, someone who understands how much it means to see your favorite band live.

We handle 90% of our customer support over email, so the successful candidate will understand exactly what it takes to provide the best service possible through this channel. On the one hand, we’re looking for a wordsmith extraordinaire, a stickler for grammar and a torch-bearer for Songkick’s tone and principles. Equally, the right person will have an epic track record of problem solving, increasing efficiency and managing a team.

We pride ourselves on providing exceptional support for our users. We need someone who’ll continue to push the bar on these high standards, who’s creative about going above and beyond to make things right, and who’ll instill these values throughout the department and the wider company.

He or she will lead Songkick’s Customer Support team in the UK, helping to ensure that every touchpoint with the Songkick service is an opportunity to reflect positively on the company, increase our fan base and raise awareness of the Songkick brand.

Application deadline: 25 November 2016

Responsibilities

  • Overseeing the day-to-day operations of Songkick’s UK support team
  • Managing the support queue, monitoring response times and triaging with support reps when necessary
  • Leading case QA, ensuring that the team are responding consistently and at a high standard
  • Being available for high level case escalation, working to resolve refunds, official complaints and gnarly or sensitive situations
  • Conceiving, overseeing and driving forward process improvements, identifying opportunities to improve and streamline, and helping to implement where required
  • Understanding the inner workings of the department, and identifying opportunities to make the team more efficient - at both a broad and granular level
  • Work on facilitating efficient and effective communications with other departments, and looking at areas where we can improve
  • Overseeing project management, identifying project areas for the support team and monitoring progress through quarters
  • Managing fires, being the point person with tech and delegating tasks that manifest as a result
  • Monitoring support health, reporting on weekly metrics (regionally) and working with the head of the department to identify and react to trends and issues
  • Vocalising the department’s achievements, being the cheerleader on Slack, email, at company events and other meetings for what we’re working on and what we’ve accomplished
  • Managing team health, running weekly 1:1s checking in on happiness, career development, project progress and roadblocks
  • Assisting with the department’s hiring process, from candidate to selection to conducting interviews
  • Overseeing the department’s onboarding and training process, ensuring that new hires understand the Songkick landscape, are in tune with our goals and principles, and are being trained thoroughly and developing in-line with expectations

Requirements

  • Educated to degree level
  • Three or more years’ experience in a related field - previous work in a technology or startup environment a strong plus
  • Experience managing a small team
  • Energetic, passionate, and driven individual who can easily collaborate with others
  • Sensitive to varied customer personalities
  • Flawless writing skills
  • Strong work ethic and sense of ownership
  • At ease when communicating with others, whether that’s via email, phone or face-to-face
  • Dedicated to team and personal success
  • Flexible and adaptable in a fast workplace environment
  • Comfortable taking initiative and making decisions independent of others
  • Flexibility to work occasional early mornings/evenings and weekend shifts when required (weekend shifts are rotated throughout the team)
  • An ambassador for an honest, open and conscientious team culture

Big plus if you have

  • Knowledge of ticketing and/or live music experience
  • Knowledge of social media use for customer service
  • Previous experience with a customer support platform such as Desk or Zendesk

Culture

Songkick connects some of the world’s greatest artists with millions of music fans around the globe. As the world's leading independent artist-ticketing and concert discovery platform, Songkick partners with hundreds of artists to take control of the ticketing experience and engage with fans in incredible ways. The Songkick vision is simple: every show should sell out.

Used by millions, its concert discovery platform is the world’s biggest direct-to-fan marketing channel, targeting the people who listen to music across more than 60 markets globally with notifications and tickets whenever their favorite artists are playing nearby. With a prioritized effort to empower artists to take control of their ticketing, Songkick uses its platform to better the fan experience, and its proprietary technology to ensure tickets end up in the hands of dedicated fans, rather than on the secondary market.
